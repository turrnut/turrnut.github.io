THE CONSTITUTION OF THE TURRNUT REPUBLIC

Last updated on November 14, 2023

Chapter One: Preamble

In acknowledgment of the remarkable influence and revolutionary essence of the Turrnut Republic, we, the collective members, assemble to establish a foundational principle from which our shared ideals shall flourish, and our community shall be enriched. We hold the following truths to be self-evident, that the Turrnut Republic is founded upon these principles that shall guide our actions and our future.

Chapter Two: Chapter Two: Rights of the People

The citizens of the Republic have the following basic rights that shall not be infringed:

The citizens of the Republic shall possess the inherent and inviolable right to petition the government for the redress of grievances, seeking justice, and expressing concerns. This sacred right shall remain unassailable;
The citizens of the Republic are granted the right to remain silent, should they choose to do so, in the face of any inquiry or accusation. No individual shall be compelled to speak against their will, protecting their autonomy and preserving the presumption of innocence;
The citizens of the Republic shall have the unassailable right to express their thoughts, ideas, and opinions without fear of censorship or reprisal. This freedom extends to all forms of communication, including but not limited to speech, writing, and assembly;
The citizens of the Republic possess the solemn right to critique and hold accountable those in positions of authority, including staff members of the Republic. Constructive criticism and the expression of discontent shall be protected as a vital aspect of our commitment to accountability, transparency, and the pursuit of the common good;
The citizens of the Republic shall be treated with absolute equality, irrespective of their race, ethnicity, gender, sexuality, or religion. Discrimination in any form is expressly prohibited, and the government shall take affirmative measures to ensure that all individuals enjoy equal opportunities and protection under the law.

These rights are deemed fundamental to the people of the Republic, for without them the Republic would be tyrannical and oppressive.

Chapter Three: Responsibilities of the People

While the citizens of the Republic have certain rights, they have responsibilities. Therefore, in conjunction with the rights declared, every member of the Republic bears certain responsibilities, which are indispensable to the preservation of our Republic. These responsibilities encompass:

The responsibility to obey the Rules of the Republic, which are established to maintain order and justice within our society;
The responsibility to respect other members of the Republic, creating and encouraging an atmosphere of tolerance, cooperation, and unity;
The responsibility to speak up against unjust laws, for it is the duty of every citizen to strive for the just and equitable rule of law;
The responsibility to report any abuse of power, as transparency and accountability are essential to safeguard the principles upon which our Republic stands.

The responsibilities outlined are considered fundamental to the Republic's well-being and existence. Without the adherence to these responsibilities, the Republic may descend into chaos and anarchy, thus undermining the rights and aspirations of its citizens.

Chapter Four: How the government is elect and organized

The Turrnut Republic, a democracy, is founded upon the consent of its people. Its leaders, known as Administrators, shall be selected by the citizens.

The Administrator, the paramount figure amongst the legislative, executive, and judicial realms. They are elected on the day of June 6th and January 1st each year, known as Reelection Days. They shall also form the Jury and possess the authority to adjudicate in matters of justice. Three Administrators comprise this esteemed office, one of whom is the Republic's Owner, who, by the fundamental principles of the Constitution and Election, is not subject to electoral processes.In all legal matters, the Administrators shall serve as the Jury. They bear the responsibility of determining the guilt or innocence of the accused, upholding the principles of the Constitution.
The Moderators, possess the power of legislation, are appointed by the Administrators. While they hold the privilege to present their voices in court proceedings, they are bereft of the voting power in such matters. Their tenure may be subject to renewal under the reelection of new Administrators, with the maximum count of Moderators set at three.
One Judge, appointed by the Administrators, maintains the authority to propose the nullification of unconstitutional laws, bills, and statutes, subject to approval by over half of the Jury, composed of the Administrators. In court proceedings, the consensus of the majority serves as the ordinary path to decision-making, but the Judge possesses the exclusive ability to veto any verdict of the Jury, in cases of unconstitutionality, which may only be overruled by unanimous consent of the Jury, while also holds sway over the sentencing of those found guilty by the Administrator's Jury.
One Treasure is designated by the Administrators who is entrusted with the issuance of the Turrnut Currency, commonly referred to as Turrcoins. Furthermore, the Treasurer shoulders the solemn duty of safeguarding against the blight of inflation.

The power of the Turrnut Republic shall be split into three branches:
The Legislative Branch, comprising Mods and Administrators.
The Executive Branch, comprising The Treasurer and Administrators.
The Judicial Branch, comprising The Judge and the Jury(the Administrators)

Chapter Five: How amendments can be made

The Constitution of the Turrnut Republic is the founding principle of our nation. Yet in recognition of the evolving needs of our government and its citizens, Amendments can be made to make the Constitution adaptive. An Amendment can only be made when two-thirds consent is achieved in the Legislative Branch, otherwise, the amendment shall not be passed.
